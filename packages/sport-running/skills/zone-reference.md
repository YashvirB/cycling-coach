# Running Pace-Zone Reference

Six pace zones, all anchored on the athlete's **Critical Speed (CS)** — the asymptote of the speed–duration relationship and the heavy↔severe boundary (the maximal metabolic steady state). Each band edge is a fraction of CS.

**Only two edges are literature-grounded** (cite-grade): the **0.823 × CS** lower boundary (aerobic threshold / LT1; Hunter 2024 meta-analysis, 95% CI 81.1–83.6) and **1.0 × CS** itself (heavy↔severe / MMSS; Nixon 2021). The other edges are a coaching convention for spacing the bands — useful, but not to be quoted as measured thresholds.

| Zone | Fraction of CS | Feel / RPE | Use |
|------|----------------|-----------|-----|
| Z1 Recovery | < 0.72 | RPE 1–2, all-day easy | Active recovery; clears fatigue, no real aerobic stimulus |
| Z2 Easy | 0.72 – 0.823 | RPE 3–4, full-sentence talk | Aerobic-base bulk of the week, below the aerobic threshold |
| Z3 Moderate | 0.823 – 0.91 | RPE 5–6, short phrases | Steady heavy-domain running; the grey zone — spend it deliberately |
| Z4 Threshold | 0.91 – 1.0 | RPE 7–8, comfortably hard | Tempo-to-threshold approaching the sustainable ceiling at CS |
| Z5 VO2max | 1.0 – 1.12 | RPE 9, no talking | Above CS, no steady state; 3–8 min VO2 intervals |
| Z6 Anaerobic | > 1.12 | RPE 10, all-out | Short speed reps on anaerobic capacity; duration-limited, not CS-prescribed |

## How to present zones

- State the two anchor definitions: lower = 82.3% of CS (LT1), upper = CS (MMSS).
- Frame paces as **RPE-checked estimates**, not lab numbers. If pace and effort disagree, trust effort.
- Disclose the CS source (platform-reported vs coach-entered) the tool returns.
- The lower boundary is flat for everyone; a coach/athlete may override it within 0.78–0.88 of CS, and an out-of-range override is clamped and disclosed. No fitness-graded factor, no sex coefficient.

## Manual override

If the athlete or coach has a better aerobic-threshold estimate than the flat 82.3%, pass it as a fraction of CS. It is clamped to [0.78, 0.88] to keep "easy" from creeping into the threshold neighbourhood; the clamp is reported back so the athlete sees what was applied.
